# Log-Rank Loss

The log-rank loss is motivated by the log-rank test, which compares survival curves between groups.

## Log-Rank Test (Background)

The log-rank test evaluates whether two survival curves are statistically different by comparing observed vs. expected events at each time point. It is the most common test for comparing KM curves.

## As a Loss Function

The log-rank loss encourages the model to produce risk scores that maximally separate high-risk from low-risk patients in terms of survival curves.

```
L = -Σ_t [ O_h(t) - E_h(t) ]
```

where O_h and E_h are observed and expected events in the high-risk group at time t.

## Properties

- Differentiable approximations are needed for gradient-based optimization
- Directly optimizes a clinically meaningful objective (group separation)
- Less common than Cox PH loss in practice, but useful when stratification is the primary goal

## Implementation

The log-rank **test** is off-the-shelf in `lifelines` — use it to *evaluate* a stratification, not to train (see [`references/6.2_lifelines.md`](../references/6.2_lifelines.md)):

```python
from lifelines.statistics import logrank_test

result = logrank_test(T_high, T_low, E_high, E_low)
print(result.test_statistic, result.p_value)
```

The log-rank **loss** is different: the test is non-differentiable (it depends on a hard high/low group assignment), so a training loss needs a smooth relaxation — typically a softmax/sigmoid over the risk score in place of the discrete group indicator. This is not provided by the classical packages (`lifelines`, `scikit-survival`); it is a custom loss in deep stratification pipelines such as the SSL3D-Survival model in [`07_ssl3d_tutorial/`](../07_ssl3d_tutorial/7.1_overview.md).

> **When to reach for it.** Cox/NLL optimize ranking or likelihood; the log-rank loss optimizes the *thing the clinician sees* — separation of KM curves between risk groups. Use it (often as an auxiliary term) when group stratification, not individual risk, is the deliverable. The cutpoint it implies connects directly to [`04_thresholds/4.3_max_log_rank_cutoff.md`](../04_thresholds/4.3_max_log_rank_cutoff.md).
