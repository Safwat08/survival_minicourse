# Loss Functions Compared

Modules [`3.1`](3.1_cox_ph_loss.md)–[`3.4`](3.4_deephit_loss.md) each cover one loss in depth. This page is the cross-cutting view: how they trade off, what they assume, what they output, and how to pick one. Use it as a cheat-sheet; follow the links for the full treatment of any single loss.

The four families:

- **Cox PH** ([`3.1`](3.1_cox_ph_loss.md)) — ranks subjects via the partial likelihood; leaves the baseline hazard unspecified.
- **Parametric / discrete NLL** ([`3.2`](3.2_negative_log_likelihood_loss.md)) — writes the time distribution down explicitly and maximizes its likelihood.
- **Log-rank** — maximizes the separation between risk-group survival curves (summarized in the tables below; no dedicated module yet).
- **DeepHit** ([`3.4`](3.4_deephit_loss.md)) — a discrete joint distribution over time and event type; likelihood + ranking, competing risks native.

## At a glance

| Loss | What it optimizes | Primary output | Key assumption | Competing risks | Typical package |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Cox PH** (DeepSurv) | Ranking — order of events (partial likelihood) | Risk score (log-hazard); $S(t)$ via Breslow | **Proportional hazards** | No (single event) | lifelines, scikit-survival, pycox, torchsurv |
| **Parametric NLL** | Likelihood under a chosen distribution | Full $S(t)$ + distribution parameters | **A distributional form** (Weibull, …) | No¹ | lifelines, torchsurv |
| **Discrete-time NLL** | Likelihood over binned hazards | Hazard/PMF per time bin → $S(t)$ | Time **binning** (no PH, no distribution) | No¹ | pycox (LogisticHazard, MTLR, PMF), Nnet-survival |
| **Log-rank** | Separation of risk-group KM curves | Risk score / group split | None specific (needs smooth relaxation) | No | custom (test in lifelines) |
| **DeepHit** | Likelihood **+** ranking (weighted) | Discrete joint PMF over (time, event) → $S(t)$, CIF | None (no PH, no distribution); binning | **Yes, native** | pycox (DeepHit, DeepHitSingle) |
| **DSM** (parametric, deep) | Likelihood under a *mixture* of distributions | Full $S(t)$, CIF | Mixture of parametric shapes | Yes | auton-survival |

¹ Single-event by default; the same likelihood machinery extends to competing risks (DeepHit, DSM).

## Assumptions & flexibility

The core trade-off in survival modeling is **how much structure you impose on time**. More structure → fewer parameters, smoother extrapolation, but wrong if the assumption is violated.

| Loss | Proportional hazards? | Distributional shape? | Time axis |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cox PH** | **Required** | None (baseline free) | Continuous, *order only* |
| **Parametric NLL** | Implied by the chosen family | **Fixed** (you pick it) | Continuous, closed-form |
| **Discrete-time NLL** | Not required | None | Discrete bins |
| **Log-rank** | Not required | None | Continuous (event times) |
| **DeepHit** | Not required | None | Discrete bins |
| **DSM** | Not required | Mixture (flexible) | Continuous, closed-form |

> **Reading this table.** Cox buys freedom on the *shape of risk over time* (no distribution) by paying with the *proportional-hazards* constraint (see [`3.1`](3.1_cox_ph_loss.md#the-proportional-hazards-assumption)). Parametric NLL is the mirror image: it drops PH but commits to a distributional shape ([`3.2`](3.2_negative_log_likelihood_loss.md#common-distributions)). DeepHit and discrete-time NLL drop *both* — at the cost of discretizing time and needing more data.

## Output & evaluation

What a loss produces dictates how you evaluate it.

| Loss | Output type | Extrapolate past last event? | Natural metric |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cox PH** | Ranking (risk score) | No (baseline ends with data) | [C-index](../05_metrics/5.1_c_index.md) (rank-based) |
| **Parametric NLL** | Absolute $S(t)$ probabilities | **Yes** (formula continues) | Brier score, calibration, likelihood |
| **Discrete-time NLL** | $S(t)$ on the bin grid | No (limited to grid) | C-index, Brier (binned) |
| **Log-rank** | Group separation | No | Log-rank statistic / $p$-value |
| **DeepHit** | $S(t)$ + cause-specific CIF | No (limited to grid) | C-index + Brier |
| **DSM** | Absolute $S(t)$, CIF | **Yes** | C-index, Brier, calibration |

> **Ranking vs. probability.** A *risk score* (Cox, log-rank) only orders patients — evaluate with the rank-based [C-index](../05_metrics/5.1_c_index.md). A *probability* model (parametric NLL, DeepHit, DSM) predicts an absolute $S(t)$ — also check **calibration** (do 70%-survival predictions actually survive 70% of the time?) and the **Brier score**.

## Variants cheat-sheet

The deep-learning literature mostly varies *within* these families:

| Family | Variant | What changes |
|---|---|---|
| **Cox** | `CoxPH` / DeepSurv | Plain deep Cox |
| | `CoxCC` | Case-control sampling — SGD-friendly, large data ([`3.1`](3.1_cox_ph_loss.md#key-takeaways-for-applied-ml-scientists)) |
| | `CoxTime` | Drops proportional hazards (time-dependent effects) |
| **NLL (parametric)** | Weibull AFT | Single parametric distribution per subject |
| | DSM | *Mixture* of distributions; competing risks |
| **NLL (discrete)** | `LogisticHazard` / Nnet-survival | Hazard per bin |
| | `MTLR` | Multi-task logistic regression over bins |
| | `PMF` | Directly models the per-bin probability mass |
| **DeepHit** | `DeepHitSingle` | One event type |
| | `DeepHit` | Competing risks |

## How to choose

Walk down these questions in order; the first "yes" usually points to your loss.

1. **Are there competing risks** (multiple mutually exclusive event types)? → **DeepHit** ([`3.4`](3.4_deephit_loss.md)) or **DSM**. See [`1.4`](../01_background/1.4_multi_events_and_competing_risks.md).
2. **Is the deliverable group stratification** (split patients into risk groups whose KM curves separate)? → **Log-rank loss**, often as an auxiliary term. Connects to [`4.3`](../04_thresholds/4.3_max_log_rank_cutoff.md).
3. **Do you need absolute, calibrated $S(t)$ — or to extrapolate beyond follow-up**? → **Parametric NLL** ([`3.2`](3.2_negative_log_likelihood_loss.md)) or **DSM**.
4. **Do you suspect non-proportional hazards** (effects that flip or fade over time)? → **DeepHit**, **discrete-time NLL**, or **CoxTime** — avoid plain Cox.
5. **Otherwise — you just need a solid risk ranking?** → **Cox PH** ([`3.1`](3.1_cox_ph_loss.md)). It's the default for a reason: no distributional assumption, strong baseline, well-tooled.

> **Rule of thumb.** Start with **Cox** for a ranking baseline. Move to a **parametric/discrete NLL** when you need real probabilities or non-PH flexibility. Reach for **DeepHit/DSM** when competing risks are in play. Add a **log-rank** term when the clinical output is risk *groups*, not individual scores.
