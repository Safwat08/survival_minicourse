# Loss Functions Compared

Modules [`2.1`](2.1_cox_ph_loss.md)–[`2.4`](2.4_deephit_loss.md) each cover one loss in depth. This page is the cross-cutting view: how they trade off, what they assume, what they output, and how to pick one. Use it as a cheat-sheet; follow the links for the full treatment of any single loss.

The three families:

- **Cox PH** ([`2.1`](2.1_cox_ph_loss.md)) — ranks subjects via the partial likelihood; leaves the baseline hazard unspecified.
- **Parametric / discrete NLL** ([`2.2`](2.2_negative_log_likelihood_loss.md)) — writes the time distribution down explicitly and maximizes its likelihood.
- **DeepHit** ([`2.4`](2.4_deephit_loss.md)) — a discrete joint distribution over time and event type; likelihood + ranking, competing risks native.

## At a glance

| Loss | What it optimizes | Primary output | Key assumption | Competing risks | Typical package |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Cox PH** (DeepSurv) | Partial likelihood over risk sets | Relative-risk score; $S(t)$ after baseline estimation | **Proportional hazards** | One cause-specific fit at a time | lifelines, scikit-survival, pycox, torchsurv |
| **Parametric NLL** | Likelihood under a chosen distribution | Full $S(t)$ + distribution parameters | **A distributional form** (Weibull, …) | No¹ | lifelines, torchsurv |
| **Discrete-time NLL** | Likelihood over binned hazards | Hazard/PMF per time bin → $S(t)$ | Time **binning** (no PH, no distribution) | No¹ | pycox (LogisticHazard, MTLR, PMF), Nnet-survival |
| **DeepHit** | Likelihood **+** ranking (weighted) | Discrete joint PMF over (time, event) → $S(t)$, CIF | Time binning; no PH or named distribution | **Yes, native** | pycox (DeepHit, DeepHitSingle) |
| **DSM** (parametric, deep) | Likelihood under a *mixture* of distributions | Full $S(t)$, CIF | Mixture of parametric shapes | Yes | auton-survival |

¹ Single-event by default; the same likelihood machinery extends to competing risks (DeepHit, DSM).

## Assumptions & flexibility

The core trade-off in survival modeling is **how much structure you impose on time**. More structure → fewer parameters, smoother extrapolation, but wrong if the assumption is violated.

| Loss | Proportional hazards? | Distributional shape? | Time axis |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cox PH** | **Required** | None (baseline free) | Continuous, *order only* |
| **Parametric NLL** | Depends on the regression parameterization; not inherent to NLL | **Fixed family** (you pick it) | Continuous, often closed-form |
| **Discrete-time NLL** | Not required | None | Discrete bins |
| **DeepHit** | Not required | None | Discrete bins |
| **DSM** | Not required | Mixture (flexible) | Continuous, closed-form |

> **Reading this table.** Cox leaves the baseline hazard shape unspecified but
> imposes proportional hazards. Parametric NLL commits to a distributional family;
> whether it also implies PH depends on how covariates enter that family. DeepHit
> and discrete-time NLL avoid both restrictions but still depend on the chosen time
> grid and model capacity.

## Output & evaluation

What a loss produces dictates how you evaluate it.

| Loss | Output type | Extrapolate past last event? | Natural metric |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cox PH** | Risk score; $S(t)$ after baseline estimation | No reliable baseline extrapolation | C-index; Brier/calibration if $S(t)$ is used |
| **Parametric NLL** | Absolute $S(t)$ probabilities | **Yes** (formula continues) | Brier score, calibration, likelihood |
| **Discrete-time NLL** | $S(t)$ on the bin grid | No (limited to grid) | C-index, Brier (binned) |
| **DeepHit** | $S(t)$ + cause-specific CIF | No (limited to grid) | C-index + Brier |
| **DSM** | Absolute $S(t)$, CIF | **Yes** | C-index, Brier, calibration |

> **Ranking vs. probability.** Evaluate a bare risk score with discrimination
> metrics such as the [C-index](../03_metrics/3.1_c_index.md). Whenever a model is
> used to report $S(t)$ or a CIF—including Cox after baseline estimation—also assess
> calibration and overall probabilistic error with the Brier score.

## Variants cheat-sheet

The deep-learning literature mostly varies *within* these families:

| Family | Variant | What changes |
|---|---|---|
| **Cox** | `CoxPH` / DeepSurv | Plain deep Cox |
| | `CoxCC` | Case-control sampling — SGD-friendly, large data ([`2.1`](2.1_cox_ph_loss.md#key-takeaways-for-applied-ml-scientists)) |
| | `CoxTime` | Drops proportional hazards (time-dependent effects) |
| **NLL (parametric)** | Weibull AFT | Single parametric distribution per subject |
| | DSM | *Mixture* of distributions; competing risks |
| **NLL (discrete)** | `LogisticHazard` / Nnet-survival | Hazard per bin |
| | `MTLR` | Multi-task logistic regression over bins |
| | `PMF` | Directly models the per-bin probability mass |
| **DeepHit** | `DeepHitSingle` | One event type |
| | `DeepHit` | Competing risks |

## How to choose

Walk down these questions in order; the first "yes" usually points to your loss.

1. **Are there competing risks** (multiple mutually exclusive event types)? → Start with descriptive Aalen–Johansen and cause-specific or Fine–Gray baselines; consider **DeepHit** ([`2.4`](2.4_deephit_loss.md)) or **DSM** when a flexible joint prediction model is justified. See [`1.4`](../01_background/1.4_multi_events_and_competing_risks.md).
2. **Do you need smooth, model-based extrapolation beyond follow-up, with a defensible distributional assumption?** → **Parametric NLL** ([`2.2`](2.2_negative_log_likelihood_loss.md)) or **DSM**. Validate extrapolation separately.
3. **Do you suspect non-proportional hazards** (effects that flip or fade over time)? → **DeepHit**, **discrete-time NLL**, or **CoxTime** — avoid plain Cox.
4. **Otherwise — you just need a solid risk ranking?** → **Cox PH** ([`2.1`](2.1_cox_ph_loss.md)). It's the default for a reason: no distributional assumption, strong baseline, well-tooled.

> **Rule of thumb.** Start with a well-validated **Cox** baseline. Move to a
> parametric or discrete NLL when its time representation matches the clinical
> question. Consider DeepHit or DSM for competing risks, but compare them with
> simpler cause-specific and CIF-based baselines.
