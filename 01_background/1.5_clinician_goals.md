# Clinician Goals in Survival Analysis

Survival models can translate imaging and clinical data into prognostic outputs.
Whether those outputs should change care requires external validation, assessment of
clinical utility, and comparison with current practice—not predictive accuracy alone.

## Risk Score

A continuous score that ranks patients by their estimated risk. Higher score = higher hazard. Used to compare patients or cohorts.

- Output of Cox PH (the proportional-hazards model defined in [`2.1`](../02_loss_functions/2.1_cox_ph_loss.md)): a *linear predictor*, $\beta^\top x$. Relative to the reference covariate vector, $\exp(\beta^\top x)$ is a hazard ratio. A positive score is higher risk than that reference and a negative score is lower; the score is **not necessarily centered at zero in the study cohort**.
- Output of deep models (e.g. DeepSurv, the neural Cox of [`2.1`](../02_loss_functions/2.1_cox_ph_loss.md)): a **scalar** risk score per patient — i.e. *one single number* for each patient (not a list or a curve), the network's $\varphi(x)$ standing in for the linear $\beta^\top x$. Like the linear predictor it only *ranks* patients; it is not a probability. For absolute probabilities you need a full S(t) curve — see "Event Probability by Time" below.

**Symbols used above** (all defined in full where the concept lives):

| Symbol | Meaning | Defined in |
|---|---|---|
| $x$ | the patient's *covariate vector* — their features stacked into a list, e.g. `[age, tumor_size, biomarker]` | [`2.1`](../02_loss_functions/2.1_cox_ph_loss.md) |
| $\beta$ | the *coefficient vector* — one learned weight per feature | [`2.1`](../02_loss_functions/2.1_cox_ph_loss.md) |
| $\beta^\top x$ | the *linear predictor* — the dot product $\beta_1 x_1 + \beta_2 x_2 + \dots$, a single number ($\top$ = transpose, **not** the survival time $T$) | [`2.1`](../02_loss_functions/2.1_cox_ph_loss.md) |
| $\exp(\beta^\top x)$ | the *hazard ratio* — how many times the patient's hazard is vs. baseline ($>1$ higher, $<1$ lower) | [`2.1`](../02_loss_functions/2.1_cox_ph_loss.md) |
| $\varphi(x)$ | the neural-network risk score that replaces $\beta^\top x$ in deep models | [`2.1`](../02_loss_functions/2.1_cox_ph_loss.md) (*In Deep Learning*) |
| $S(t)$ | the *survival function* — P(still event-free past time $t$) | [`1.1`](../01_background/1.1_what_is_survival_analysis.md) |

## Risk Stratification

Divides patients into groups (for example, low / intermediate / high) using a
pre-specified or previously validated threshold on the risk score. Stratification
can support trial enrichment, reporting, and discussions about follow-up intensity,
but a prognostic group alone does not establish that a treatment helps.

See `03_metrics/` for methods to select the cutoff.

## Event Probability by Time

Estimates the probability that a patient experiences the event by a specific time point — e.g., "60% probability of recurrence within 2 years."

- For a single-event endpoint, derived from the survival function: $P(T \le t) = 1-S(t)$
- More clinically interpretable than a raw risk score

With competing risks, use the cause-specific CIF rather than $1-S(t)$ for the
probability of a particular event; see [`1.4`](1.4_multi_events_and_competing_risks.md).

## Which Output Comes From Which Call

Each goal maps to a distinct prediction method — knowing which one a package exposes tells you what the model can actually deliver.

| Goal | Output | Example call |
|---|---|---|
| Risk score | scalar per patient | `model.predict(X)` (scikit-survival); `cph.predict_partial_hazard(df)` (lifelines); `log_hz = model(x)` (torchsurv) |
| Event probability by time | full S(t) curve | `model.predict_surv_df(x)` (pycox); `model.predict_survival_function(X)` (scikit-survival / lifelines) |
| Risk at fixed horizons | P(event by t) | `model.predict_risk(features, times=[8, 12, 16])` (auton-survival) |
| Risk stratification | group label | threshold the risk score — see [`03_metrics/`](../03_metrics/3.3_km_curves.md#choosing-a-risk-threshold) |

See [`04_packages/`](../04_packages/4.1_pycox.md) for each package's full API.

> **A risk score alone cannot answer "what is this patient's 2-year recurrence
> probability?"** You need an estimated $S(t)$ or, under competing risks, a CIF.
> Cox models can provide $S(t)$ after estimating the baseline survival, but those
> absolute probabilities still require calibration and external validation.
