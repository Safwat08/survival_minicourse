# Types of Events

## Recurrence

Tumor recurrence is the reappearance of cancer after treatment. Subtypes:

- **Local recurrence**: at the original tumor site
- **Regional recurrence**: in nearby lymph nodes
- **Distant recurrence / metastasis**: in other organs

Recurrence endpoints are often used in disease-free survival (DFS) or recurrence-free survival (RFS) analyses.

## Death

- **Overall survival (OS)**: time from treatment to death from any cause
- **Cancer-specific survival (CSS)**: time to death attributed to the cancer

OS is the gold standard endpoint in oncology trials. CSS requires accurate cause-of-death records and is subject to misclassification.

## Choosing the Right Endpoint

| Endpoint | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|
| OS | Unambiguous | Requires long follow-up |
| DFS / RFS | Earlier signal | Recurrence detection depends on imaging schedule |
| PFS | Usable in trials | Progression criteria can vary |
