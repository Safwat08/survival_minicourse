# Brier Score

The Brier score measures **calibration** — how close a model's predicted survival *probabilities* are to what actually happened. It is the complement of the [C-index](3.1_c_index.md): where the C-index only checks the *ordering* of patients, the Brier score checks whether the predicted numbers are *right*.

## Definition

At a fixed time $t$, the Brier score is the **mean squared error** between the predicted survival probability and the observed outcome:

$$
BS(t) \;=\; \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}\Big(
\underbrace{S(t \mid x_i)}_{\text{predicted prob. of surviving past } t}
\;-\;
\underbrace{\mathbf{1}(T_i > t)}_{\text{1 if actually survived past } t,\ \text{else } 0}
\Big)^2
$$

The indicator $\mathbf{1}(T_i > t)$ is the ground truth: 1 if subject $i$ was still event-free at $t$, 0 if the event had already happened. A perfect model predicts $S(t \mid x_i) = 1$ for survivors and $0$ for those who failed, giving $BS(t) = 0$.

> **Worked example — one time point.** Evaluate at $t = 12$ months for three patients who all survived past 12 (so each ground-truth indicator is 1):
> | Patient | Predicted $S(12)$ | Truth $\mathbf{1}(T>12)$ | Squared error |
> |---|---|---|---|
> | A | 0.9 | 1 | $0.01$ |
> | B | 0.6 | 1 | $0.16$ |
> | C | 0.8 | 1 | $0.04$ |
>
> $BS(12) = (0.01 + 0.16 + 0.04)/3 \approx 0.07$. Patient B hurts the score most — the model under-predicted a survivor's chances.

## Inverse Probability of Censoring Weighting (IPCW)

The raw score above breaks for **censored** subjects: if subject $i$ is censored before $t$, the indicator $\mathbf{1}(T_i > t)$ is unknown. The IPCW-corrected Brier score handles this by reweighting each subject by the inverse probability of being uncensored, $w_i$:

$$
BS_{\text{IPCW}}(t) \;=\; \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n} w_i \,\Big( S(t \mid x_i) - \mathbf{1}(T_i > t) \Big)^2
$$

Subjects who are *less* likely to be observed get *more* weight, so the censored data don't bias the estimate. This is the version the packages compute.

## Integrated Brier Score (IBS)

A single time point only tells part of the story. The IBS averages $BS(t)$ across a time range $[t_{\min}, t_{\max}]$:

$$
IBS \;=\; \frac{1}{t_{\max} - t_{\min}} \int_{t_{\min}}^{t_{\max}} BS(t)\, dt
$$

This is the standard one-number summary of calibration across the whole follow-up.

## Interpretation

- **Lower is better:** $0$ = perfect, $0.25$ = uninformative (a coin-flip predicting 0.5 everywhere).
- Always compare against the **Kaplan–Meier baseline** (a covariate-free model, see [`3.3`](3.3_km_curves.md)) — a useful model must beat it.

## Key takeaways for applied ML scientists

- **It's your calibration check.** Report it alongside the [C-index](3.1_c_index.md): discrimination *and* calibration. A model can ace one and fail the other.
- **Use the IPCW version with any real (censored) data** — the raw score is biased. The packages do this for you, but they need the *training* survival arrays to estimate the censoring weights.
- **Keep evaluation times inside the follow-up window** (see pitfall below) — this is the single most common way to get a meaningless Brier score.
- **Prefer the IBS for model selection**, individual $BS(t)$ values for diagnosing *when* calibration breaks down.

## Implementation

**scikit-survival** is the reference implementation. Both functions take the *training* and *test* structured arrays (for the IPCW censoring estimate) plus the model's predicted survival probabilities `S(t)` at each evaluation time (see [`04_packages/4.4_scikit_survival.md`](../04_packages/4.4_scikit_survival.md)):

```python
from sksurv.metrics import brier_score, integrated_brier_score
import numpy as np

times = np.percentile(time_test[event_test], [25, 50, 75])      # stay inside the followed-up range
surv_prob = np.row_stack([fn(times) for fn in model.predict_survival_function(X_test)])

t, bs = brier_score(y_train, y_test, surv_prob, times)          # BS at each time
ibs   = integrated_brier_score(y_train, y_test, surv_prob, times)  # single summary
```

**torchsurv** provides a differentiable/GPU version for in-loop evaluation (see [`04_packages/4.3_torchsurv.md`](../04_packages/4.3_torchsurv.md)); **auton-survival** reports IBS with bootstrapped confidence intervals (see [`04_packages/4.5_auton_survival.md`](../04_packages/4.5_auton_survival.md)).

> **Pitfall — evaluation times.** Choosing `times` beyond the last observed event makes the IPCW weights explode and the score meaningless. Keep evaluation times strictly inside the follow-up window, and never let `times` include the maximum follow-up time.

> **Brier complements, not replaces, the C-index.** A model can rank perfectly (high C-index) yet be badly calibrated (high Brier), and vice versa. Report both — discrimination *and* calibration. See [`3.1`](3.1_c_index.md).
